Personal injury FAQ

Questions people ask us first

What is my personal injury case worth?

One of the most common questions injury victims have is how much their case is worth. The value of your personal injury claim depends on several factors, including:

  • The severity of your injuries
  • The cost of medical treatment
  • The amount of lost wages
  • The long-term impact of the injury on your life
  • The impact of the accident on your mental health

Ultimately, the worth of your personal injury claim will depend on the unique details of your case. At Alpert Schreyer Personal Injury Lawyers, we offer free consultations to help you understand the potential value of your case and the legal options available to you.

How much does it cost to hire a personal injury lawyer?

One of the main concerns for people seeking legal representation after an accident is the cost. At Alpert Schreyer Personal Injury Lawyers, we work on a contingency fee basis. This means you won't have to pay any upfront legal fees, and our fee is a percentage of the compensation we recover for you. We'll go over exactly how fees and case costs work in your free consultation, before you sign anything.

This fee structure allows everyone, regardless of their financial situation, to access skilled legal representation.

How long do I have to file a lawsuit after a personal injury accident in Maryland?

In Maryland, the statute of limitations for personal injury claims is generally three years from the date of the accident. If you fail to file within this time, you may lose your right to compensation.

Can I recover compensation if I'm being blamed for an accident in Maryland?

Maryland follows a strict contributory negligence rule. If you are found even 1% at fault, you may be barred from recovering compensation.

What kinds of damages are available to personal injury victims?

Personal injury victims may be entitled to economic damages (medical bills, lost wages) and non-economic damages (pain and suffering, emotional distress). In rare cases, punitive damages may also be awarded in Maryland if actual malice is proven.
